Sightseeing spots

Gyeongbokgung

Gyeongbokgung is an old palace built in 1395. It is the largest of the five great royal palaces in Seoul. In addition, the palace with excellent architectural beauty is highly evaluated. There are many cultural properties in Kagefukumiya.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon Hanok Village is an area where traditional Korean houses that have remained since the Joseon Dynasty are concentrated.

Changdeokgung

Gyeongbokgung Palace is a palace built in 1405 as a detached palace of Gyeongbokgung Palace, which is the main palace. The royal palace where the affairs of the Joseon Dynasty were held for about 270 years. There are 13 palaces on the site of about 135,000 square meters in total, and 28 towers in the back garden, which occupies the northern half of the building.
